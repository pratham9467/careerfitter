What is the FIT Score?

Your FIT Scores are numerical values that reflect how closely the characteristics of a particular career or job align with your personality strengths. A higher FIT score indicates a career that will utilize more of your natural strengths, improving your ability to make the right career choice. The FIT Score technology is exclusive to CareerFitter and requires both CareerFitter's work personality assessment test (offered as the free career test) and aptitude assessment, also known as the A3 test. What is the best type of career test for me?

The best assessments are backed by scientific research and schools of psychology. Since there are many types of career tests, the first step is determining what you would like to discover about yourself. Then, choose the test or assessment that evaluates that area of your need.

Other types of career tests and job assessments:

Desire tests help you find careers that fit your desires. They give insights into your desires and interests by helping you organize your thoughts. They often ask you questions about the requirements or conditions of a career or job, and if that requirement or condition sounds desirable to you in a work environment.

Aptitude testing helps determine your natural aptitudes, including skills, abilities, and qualities necessary to perform the functions of a job effectively. These assessments can also evaluate specific cognitive abilities such as problem-solving, critical thinking, numerical reasoning, verbal comprehension, and spatial awareness.

Personality tests use questions typically designed by psychological research to determine your general personality outside a working environment. These types of tests help you understand a more holistic view of your personality and how you will interact with others even when you are not working.

IQ tests measure intellectual ability by evaluating cognitive skills such as memory, problem-solving, and processing speed, which directly contribute to one's level of intelligence. It's important to note that while intelligence can be enhanced through learning and education, IQ tests only measure a specific subset of cognitive abilities and may not capture all aspects of intelligence.
